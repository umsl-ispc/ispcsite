ISPC Constitution

Article I

This organization shall be known as the UM-St. Louis MIS Student Programming Club. (Hereinafter known as the Club)

Article II

Purpose and Goals: The purpose of the Club is to give students at UM-St. Louis real world examples of how programming is applied in business applications; to encourage and foster further understanding and knowledge of programming principles and techniques; and to provide a forum for professional and social contact between and among students and practitioners of business applications programming. Guest speakers from leading software development companies will help accomplish the Club's goals through industry presentations and workshops.

Tutorial sessions will be provided on the topics of interest as well as areas of study within the UM-St. Louis MIS curriculum of courses. Club activities will be thoroughly documented through the use of the Club's web site. Club members will be able to access presentation materials as well as other relevant information from the on-line library maintained by the Club. The Club will foster relationships between UM-St. Louis and the corporate world by organizing joint events such as programming competitions and social events with participation of academic and corporate communities. UM-St. Louis students will benefit from the Club's activities through the increased knowledge of real world technological developments and additional networking opportunities leading to successful employment opportunities.

Article III

Membership Requirements and Limitations: Membership is open to all UM-St. Louis students, faculty, alumni and other individuals at the discretion of the club officers, who support the Club's goals and purposes. Member in Good Standing Club members who attend at least one meeting per semester shall remain a member in good standing. Members who are in good standing shall be listed on the Club's web site and allowed links to their own web sites through the Club's Web site. Only members in good standing will be allowed to vote.

Article IV

Officers: All officers shall be members in good standing of the Club and at least a part-time student at the University of Missouri-Saint Louis. Officer positions shall be as follows: President, Vice President and Web Master Club members will elect officers to positions through nominations. All nominations must have a second to the nomination. Individuals may nominate themselves, however another member of the Club must make a second motion to the nomination. Club members shall vote on candidates for officer positions. Those candidates receiving the majority of votes at the end of the winter semester shall take office the following fall semester. All officers shall serve minimum of two semesters starting from the fall semester of each academic year. No officer shall serve more than two terms for a particular position. If an officer cannot complete his/her term, the remaining officers shall appoint a member to the position to serve until the next vote at the end of the winter semester. If the officer position of the President is vacated, the Vice President shall take over the President's duties for the remaining time and the President shall appoint an officer to the open position.

Officer Positions

President Responsibilities: The President will be responsible for overseeing the activities of the Club. Additional duties will include scheduling guest speakers, organizing meeting agendas and arranging social activities.

Vice President Responsibilities: The Vice President will be responsible for running Club meetings. In addition the Vice President will act as the SGA representative for the Club. The Vice President shall take over the President's duties should the acting President not be able to perform such said duties.

Web Master Responsibilities: The Web Master will be responsible for updating the Club's web site. This will include giving club members access to topics covered by guest speakers as well as any programming projects created by the club. Additional duties will include keeping the minutes for each meeting. In addition to the above mentioned duties, the officers of the Club may perform other activities that conform to the Club's goals and purposes and do not violate the current Constitution.

Article V

Meetings

Purpose: The purpose of the club meetings will be to bring students together to learn and discuss new programming technologies as they are being applied in the work place. These meetings will foster a better understanding of existing and emerging technologies, as well as allow for open discussion on the topics covered.

Frequency: Meetings will be held once a month. The night of the meeting will be determined at the last meeting each semester to determine what night will work best with the majority of members class schedules. Meetings will be held at night so that both graduate and undergraduate students may attend.

Calling meetings: Officers of the club will call all meetings. However, meetings called must be posted on the Club's web site. For each meeting, the place, date, time and agenda for the meeting will be posted.

Article VI

Faculty Advisors: The faculty advisor shall be a member of the UM-St. Louis MIS faculty. This member should have a programming background and be able to help guide the club officers in bringing current programming topics to club members.

Duties of the Faculty Advisor are as follows:

Provide continuity from year to year as student leadership and personnel change.
Promote good student/faculty relationships
Help maintain University standards in all activities.
Exercise financial supervision
Represent Club interests to the faculty and administration

The faculty advisor shall remain in the role as long as he/she is able and willing to fulfill his/her duties to the club. If the faculty advisor wishes to step down from his/her role, they must assist the club officers in finding a new and suitable advisor within the MIS area.

Article VII

Ratification and Amendments
Club members shall vote on any ratification or proposed amendments to the Club Constitution and By Laws.
No vote shall take place unless a quorum of the chapter is present. A quorum of the chapter shall be defined as 20 percent of the voting membership of the club or at least 10 members whichever is greater. A simple majority (50%) of the members present and voting shall be required to carry a motion.
