November 16, 7.00 pm – 8.30 pm in ESH 107

Speaker: Ms. Elonka Dunin, Executive Producer and General Manager of Online Community, Simutronics Corporation
Topic: Kryptos: The Unsolved Enigma at the center of CIA Headquarters

Abstract
At the center of CIA Headquarters is a sculpture entitled Kryptos. With hundreds of characters carved into copper sheets, it carries a series of ciphers. Three of the four have been solved, but the fourth, a section with 97 characters at the very bottom, remains uncracked, and remains one of the most famous unsolved codes in the world. Cryptographer Elonka Dunin (namesake for the "Nola Kaye" character in Dan Brown's novel "The Lost Symbol"), one of the few people from outside the CIA to have seen the sculpture in person, will speak about the parts that have been solved, the famous unsolved section, and the process of solving other encrypted sculptures created by the same artist, Jim Sanborn.

Biography of Ms. Elonka Dunin
Elonka Dunin is an executive in the game industry, cofounder and Chairperson Emerita of the International Game Developers Association's Online Games SIG, and co-Director of the Global Game Jam. She is also an expert on the world's most famous unsolved codes, author of The Mammoth Book of Secret Codes and Cryptograms, and maintains a popular cryptography-related website at elonka.com which has received millions of visitors. Bestselling author Dan Brown honored Elonka by naming one of the characters in his Da Vinci Code sequel "The Lost Symbol" after her.  "Nola Kaye" is an anagrammed form of "Elonka". Elonka's company, Simutronics (play.net), is a leading developer of online multiplayer games. One of the Simutronics games, GemStone, is the longest-running such game in the world, with a history that goes back over twenty years. Other games and products include DragonRealms, Online Game of the Year CyberStrike, Fantasy University, Tiny Heroes, the game development platform HeroEngine, and the new iPhone game "Tiny Heroes".

Born in Los Angeles, Dunin studied astronomy at UCLA and then joined the United States Air Force, where she served proudly for six years. She is a world-traveler who speaks several languages, has visited scores of countries around the world, and been to every continent, including Antarctica. She is also an avid Wikipedia editor, was elected as an administrator in 2007, and has been a significant contributor to over 400 articles, especially in topics related to the Crusades and medieval history.
